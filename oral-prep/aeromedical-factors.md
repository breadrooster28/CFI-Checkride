# Aeromedical Factors

Total questions: 10

## 1. A student says they took Benadryl last night and feel “mostly fine.” What do you do?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task A: Aeromedical Factors
- **Model answer:** Do not fly until risk is acceptable; diphenhydramine is sedating and can impair performance beyond perceived effects. Use IMSAFE, discuss conservative no-fly interval, and reschedule if any impairment remains.
- **FAA reference:** PHAK FAA-H-8083-25 Ch. 17; FAA medication guidance
- **Gotcha / DPE follow-up:** Common DPE follow-up: “How do you know impairment is gone?”
- **Scenario-based:** Yes

## 2. What are the IMSAFE elements?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task A: Aeromedical Factors
- **Model answer:** Illness, Medication, Stress, Alcohol, Fatigue, Emotion/Eating. It is a preflight personal risk screen, not a one-time checkbox.
- **FAA reference:** PHAK FAA-H-8083-25 Ch. 2

## 3. What hypoxia types should pilots know and where are they most relevant?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task A: Aeromedical Factors
- **Model answer:** Hypoxic, hypemic, stagnant, and histotoxic hypoxia. In GA altitude operations, hypoxic hypoxia is most relevant as partial pressure of oxygen decreases with altitude.
- **FAA reference:** PHAK FAA-H-8083-25 Ch. 17

## 4. What are key hyperventilation signs and immediate corrective actions?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task A: Aeromedical Factors
- **Model answer:** Symptoms include lightheadedness, tingling, visual disturbance, and anxiety. Correct by consciously slowing breathing and reducing stressors while maintaining aircraft control.
- **FAA reference:** PHAK FAA-H-8083-25 Ch. 17

## 5. Explain middle-ear and sinus issues during descent and training implications.

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task A: Aeromedical Factors
- **Model answer:** Pressure cannot equalize if blocked, causing pain/disorientation and possible incapacitation. Instructors teach pressure management and reject flights with active congestion.
- **FAA reference:** PHAK FAA-H-8083-25 Ch. 17

## 6. How does carbon monoxide poisoning present in piston aircraft?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task A: Aeromedical Factors
- **Model answer:** Headache, dizziness, nausea, confusion, and cherry-red skin late. Use CO detector, open vents/windows, turn off cabin heat if suspected, and land ASAP.
- **FAA reference:** PHAK FAA-H-8083-25 Ch. 17; AFH emergency procedures

## 7. What is spatial disorientation and which illusion is common after takeoff at night?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task A: Aeromedical Factors
- **Model answer:** Spatial disorientation is conflicting sensory input leading to false attitude perception. Somatogravic illusion can cause a false nose-up sensation and dangerous push-over tendency.
- **FAA reference:** PHAK FAA-H-8083-25 Ch. 17; AFH instrument reference

## 8. How do dehydration and heat stress affect pilot performance?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task A: Aeromedical Factors
- **Model answer:** They degrade cognition, reaction time, and tolerance to workload; prevention includes hydration, cooling strategies, and conservative go/no-go decisions in hot conditions.
- **FAA reference:** PHAK FAA-H-8083-25 Ch. 17

## 9. A student had one beer 9 hours ago. Is that legal and safe?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task A: Aeromedical Factors
- **Model answer:** 14 CFR 91.17 prohibits within 8 hours and while under influence; legal minimum is not always safe. Apply IMSAFE and conservative judgment regarding residual effects.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 91.17; PHAK Ch. 17
- **Scenario-based:** Yes

## 10. How should you teach motion sickness mitigation for new students?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task A: Aeromedical Factors
- **Model answer:** Use short lessons, smooth air when possible, cool ventilation, visual horizon references, and gradual adaptation. Stop early if symptoms worsen to avoid negative conditioning.
- **FAA reference:** AIH; PHAK Ch. 17
- **Scenario-based:** Yes
