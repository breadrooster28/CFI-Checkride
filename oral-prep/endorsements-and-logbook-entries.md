# Endorsements and Logbook Entries

Total questions: 10

## 1. What regulation requires instructors to sign and maintain records of endorsements?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task G: Endorsements and Logbook Entries
- **Model answer:** CFIs must sign each student logbook endorsement and maintain records of each endorsement and training given.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 61.189

## 2. What are the three core pre-solo endorsements for airplane students?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task G: Endorsements and Logbook Entries
- **Model answer:** Aeronautical knowledge test for pre-solo, pre-solo flight training in make/model, and 90-day solo flight endorsement.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 61.87(b),(c),(n); AC 61-65H

## 3. What endorsement is required for student solo in Class B airspace?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task G: Endorsements and Logbook Entries
- **Model answer:** Specific Class B training and logbook endorsement for that Class B airspace and airports, per 61.95 with instructor determination.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 61.95; AC 61-65H

## 4. Which endorsements are needed for private pilot practical test?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task G: Endorsements and Logbook Entries
- **Model answer:** Knowledge test review endorsement and practical test endorsement certifying required training/preparedness under 61.39 and 61.103/109.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 61.39; AC 61-65H

## 5. What must a 61.31 complex/high-performance endorsement state?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task G: Endorsements and Logbook Entries
- **Model answer:** Instructor certifies person received and logged ground/flight training and is proficient to operate complex or high-performance airplane as applicable.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 61.31(e),(f); AC 61-65H

## 6. When is a tailwheel endorsement required and what does it cover?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task G: Endorsements and Logbook Entries
- **Model answer:** Required to act as PIC in a tailwheel airplane; includes normal/crosswind takeoffs/landings, wheel landings, and go-around procedures.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 61.31(i); AC 61-65H

## 7. Can you combine multiple endorsements in one logbook entry?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task G: Endorsements and Logbook Entries
- **Model answer:** Yes, if each required element is clearly documented and regulatory language intent is fully met; clarity and completeness are critical.
- **FAA reference:** AC 61-65H

## 8. A student’s 90-day solo endorsement expired yesterday but they flew today anyway. What now?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task G: Endorsements and Logbook Entries
- **Model answer:** Ground the student from further solo, document the lapse, provide remedial review as needed, and issue a new endorsement only after verifying proficiency and compliance.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 61.87(n); instructor responsibilities
- **Scenario-based:** Yes

## 9. How do you verify solo cross-country endorsement compliance before dispatch?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task G: Endorsements and Logbook Entries
- **Model answer:** Check student certificate and medical/BasicMed applicability, current pre-solo and 90-day solo endorsements, route-specific solo XC authorization, and weather/planning competency.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 61.87, 61.93; AC 61-65H
- **Scenario-based:** Yes

## 10. What endorsement does a CFI candidate need for spin training?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task G: Endorsements and Logbook Entries
- **Model answer:** A logbook endorsement from an authorized instructor certifying spin training and proficiency in instructional-level spin awareness/recovery per CFI requirements.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 61.183(i); AC 61-65H
