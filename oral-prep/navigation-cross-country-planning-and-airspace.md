# Navigation, Cross-Country Planning, and Airspace

Total questions: 10

## 1. A student pilot wants to solo to an airport 30 NM away. What endorsements are required?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task E: Navigation and Flight Planning
- **Model answer:** At minimum: student pilot solo make/model endorsement, 90-day solo endorsement, and a specific solo cross-country endorsement with route, plus repeated endorsements as required by 61.93.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 61.87, 61.93; AC 61-65H
- **Gotcha / DPE follow-up:** DPE follow-up: include repeated 90-day and route-specific requirements.
- **Scenario-based:** Yes

## 2. What is required by regulation before any flight regarding planning?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task E: Navigation and Flight Planning
- **Model answer:** Pilot in command must become familiar with all available information, including weather, fuel, alternatives, known delays, and runway lengths/performance for destination and alternates.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 91.103

## 3. How do you compute true course to heading in a nav log?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task E: Navigation and Flight Planning
- **Model answer:** Start with true course, apply variation for magnetic course, then wind correction angle for magnetic heading, and account for deviation if applicable.
- **FAA reference:** PHAK Ch. 16

## 4. What are VFR weather minimums in Class E below 10,000 MSL?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task E: Navigation and Flight Planning
- **Model answer:** 3 statute miles visibility and cloud clearance of 500 below, 1,000 above, 2,000 horizontal (3-152).
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 91.155

## 5. How do Class B, C, D, and E airspace entry requirements differ for student operations?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task E: Navigation and Flight Planning
- **Model answer:** Class B: explicit ATC clearance; Class C/D: two-way communication before entry; Class E generally no communication required for VFR but weather minima still apply. Student pilots have Class B training/endorsement limits per 61.95.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 91.131, 91.130, 91.129, 61.95

## 6. What is a Special VFR clearance and key limitations?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task E: Navigation and Flight Planning
- **Model answer:** Allows VFR within controlled surface airspace below basic VFR minima with at least 1 SM visibility and clear of clouds; at night, fixed-wing pilot must be instrument rated and aircraft IFR equipped.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 91.157
- **Gotcha / DPE follow-up:** Gotcha: student pilots generally should avoid relying on SVFR.

## 7. How do you teach lost procedures to student pilots?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task E: Navigation and Flight Planning
- **Model answer:** Use “Climb, Communicate, Confess, Comply,” plus chart/GPS cross-check, VOR radials if available, and ATC assistance early before fuel or weather margins shrink.
- **FAA reference:** PHAK Ch. 16; AIM 6

## 8. What checks verify GPS RAIM/integrity relevance in basic training?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task E: Navigation and Flight Planning
- **Model answer:** Teach database currency, annunciation monitoring, and cross-check with pilotage/dead reckoning. For IFR, integrity requirements are stricter; VFR still requires situational cross-checks.
- **FAA reference:** AIM GPS guidance; PHAK Ch. 16

## 9. A student plans direct over rising terrain with minimal altitude margin. What should you teach?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task E: Navigation and Flight Planning
- **Model answer:** Use terrain/obstruction planning with conservative altitude, checkpoint options, weather escape routes, and oxygen/performance considerations where applicable.
- **FAA reference:** PHAK Ch. 15-16; risk management
- **Scenario-based:** Yes

## 10. How are TFRs checked and briefed before flight?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task E: Navigation and Flight Planning
- **Model answer:** Use official FAA sources (NOTAMs/FSS/approved briefings), plot affected areas/times/altitudes, and develop reroutes before departure.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 91.103; NOTAM system guidance
