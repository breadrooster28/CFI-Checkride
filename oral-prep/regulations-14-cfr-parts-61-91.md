# Regulations (14 CFR Parts 61/91)

Total questions: 10

## 1. What are recent flight experience requirements to carry passengers day and night?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task F: National Airspace System and Regulations
- **Model answer:** Day: 3 takeoffs/landings within preceding 90 days in same category/class/type if required. Night: 3 takeoffs/landings to full stop during 1 hour after sunset to 1 hour before sunrise.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 61.57(a),(b)

## 2. Can a private pilot pay less than pro-rata share on a trip with passengers?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task F: National Airspace System and Regulations
- **Model answer:** No, private pilot may share only pro-rata operating expenses with passengers and must have a common purpose for the flight.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 61.113(c)

## 3. What documents and inspections are required for aircraft airworthiness currency?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task F: National Airspace System and Regulations
- **Model answer:** Required inspections include annual and, if for hire/flight instruction for hire, 100-hour; plus AD compliance and required records/equipment per applicable rules.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 91.409; 39; 91.205

## 4. What are basic VFR cloud clearance and visibility minima exceptions in Class G daytime below 1,200 AGL?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task F: National Airspace System and Regulations
- **Model answer:** Generally 1 SM and clear of clouds in many Class G daytime conditions, but instructors must teach exact table by altitude/time/airspace and use conservative personal minima.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 91.155 table

## 5. When are seat belts and shoulder harnesses required?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task F: National Airspace System and Regulations
- **Model answer:** Pilots must ensure passengers are briefed and secured during movement/takeoff/landing; shoulder harness use is required for each front seat when installed.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 91.107

## 6. What are right-of-way rules between aircraft categories?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task F: National Airspace System and Regulations
- **Model answer:** In distress has priority, then generally balloon, glider, airship, airplane/rotorcraft; converging and overtaking rules still apply, and lower aircraft on approach has right-of-way with caveats.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 91.113

## 7. What minimum safe altitudes apply over congested and non-congested areas?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task F: National Airspace System and Regulations
- **Model answer:** Over congested areas: 1,000 ft above highest obstacle within 2,000 ft; elsewhere 500 ft above surface except over open water/sparsely populated with no hazard.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 91.119

## 8. Can a student pilot act as PIC carrying a passenger?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task F: National Airspace System and Regulations
- **Model answer:** No. Student pilot limitations prohibit carrying passengers and several other operations.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 61.89

## 9. A renter asks to fly with an inoperative landing light during daytime. Is it legal?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task F: National Airspace System and Regulations
- **Model answer:** If daytime VFR and light is not required by type cert/KOEL/AD/91.205 for that operation, aircraft may be operated using 91.213(d) process (deactivate/placard and records as required).
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 91.205, 91.213
- **Scenario-based:** Yes

## 10. What are careless or reckless operation implications for instruction?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task F: National Airspace System and Regulations
- **Model answer:** Any operation endangering life/property can violate 91.13 regardless of other compliance; instructors teach conservative decision-making and intervene before risk escalates.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 91.13
