# Certificates, Documents, and Airworthiness

Total questions: 8

## 1. What personal documents must a pilot carry when acting as required crewmember?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task H: Certificates and Documents
- **Model answer:** Pilot certificate (or privileges), photo ID, and medical certificate if required for that operation.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 61.3(a)

## 2. What aircraft documents are required on board?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task H: Certificates and Documents
- **Model answer:** Airworthiness certificate, registration certificate, operating limitations (including POH/AFM placards), and current weight and balance information.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 91.203; 91.9

## 3. What makes an aircraft unairworthy even if it has a current annual?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task H: Certificates and Documents
- **Model answer:** Any unresolved condition that fails type design or safe operation standards, overdue required inspections, inoperative required equipment not handled per regulation, or noncompliance with ADs.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 3.5/21/39 principles; 91.7, 91.213

## 4. Who is responsible for determining aircraft is in condition for safe flight?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task H: Certificates and Documents
- **Model answer:** The PIC, and no person may operate an aircraft unless it is airworthy.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 91.7

## 5. How do you teach students to check AD compliance practically?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task H: Certificates and Documents
- **Model answer:** Review maintenance records for recurring and one-time AD entries, verify method of compliance and next due times/hours, and reconcile with aircraft serial/component applicability.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR Part 39; maintenance records

## 6. What are MEL vs KOEL concepts for training aircraft?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task H: Certificates and Documents
- **Model answer:** Most small training aircraft do not have an MEL. KOEL in POH lists equipment required for specific kinds of operation; otherwise use 91.213(d) process.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 91.213; POH/AFM

## 7. A checkride aircraft has an inop strobe light for day VFR. How do you evaluate legality?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task H: Certificates and Documents
- **Model answer:** Determine if required by 91.205/KOEL/type design/AD; if not required for operation, follow 91.213(d) deactivation/placard and maintenance record procedure before flight.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 91.205, 91.213
- **Scenario-based:** Yes

## 8. What is the difference between “airworthy” and “legal to dispatch” in training discussions?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task H: Certificates and Documents
- **Model answer:** Airworthy means conforming to type design and safe condition. Legal dispatch also requires regulatory compliance for the intended operation; both must be satisfied.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 91.7; 91.205; POH
