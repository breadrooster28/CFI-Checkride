# Night, High-Altitude, Emergency Operations, and Risk Management

Total questions: 10

## 1. What additional preflight planning should be emphasized for night VFR?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task I: Special Operations and Emergency Procedures
- **Model answer:** Lighting systems, weather/terrain visibility, route checkpoints, alternates, flashlight/spares, personal fatigue status, and increased reserve margins.
- **FAA reference:** PHAK Ch. 17; AFH night ops

## 2. What oxygen requirements apply above 12,500 MSL?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task I: Special Operations and Emergency Procedures
- **Model answer:** Crew must use supplemental oxygen above 12,500 MSL up to and including 14,000 MSL when over 30 minutes, and continuously above 14,000 MSL; passengers provided above 15,000 MSL.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 91.211

## 3. How does night visual illusion risk change approach technique?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task I: Special Operations and Emergency Procedures
- **Model answer:** Use stabilized approach criteria, cross-check instruments and glidepath aids, avoid black-hole illusions, and resist abrupt pitch/power changes from false cues.
- **FAA reference:** PHAK Ch. 17; AFH landing

## 4. What immediate priorities apply after engine failure after takeoff?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task I: Special Operations and Emergency Procedures
- **Model answer:** Maintain aircraft control (pitch for best glide or as appropriate), select landing area, configure/attempt restart only if altitude/time permit, and communicate/secure as able.
- **FAA reference:** AFH emergency operations; POH

## 5. How should a CFI teach emergency descent and risk tradeoffs?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task I: Special Operations and Emergency Procedures
- **Model answer:** Emphasize rapid but controlled descent, structural speed awareness, passenger considerations, terrain/traffic avoidance, and checklist discipline.
- **FAA reference:** AFH emergency procedures; POH

## 6. A learner wants to continue a night XC with a failed panel light and unfamiliar airport ahead. What do you do?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task I: Special Operations and Emergency Procedures
- **Model answer:** Apply PAVE/5P, evaluate remaining systems and workload, and generally divert to a familiar suitable airport rather than press on with degraded situational awareness.
- **FAA reference:** Risk management models; AFH
- **Scenario-based:** Yes

## 7. How do you integrate PAVE, IMSAFE, DECIDE, and 5P without making it “checklist theater”?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task I: Special Operations and Emergency Procedures
- **Model answer:** Use them at natural decision points with specific hazards, mitigations, and trigger points; tie each model to an action, not just vocabulary recitation.
- **FAA reference:** CFI ACS risk management

## 8. What is your teaching strategy for partial-power emergencies in pattern work?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task I: Special Operations and Emergency Procedures
- **Model answer:** Teach early recognition, immediate pitch and runway/field selection, conservative go-around criteria, and repetition with increasing complexity only after stable performance.
- **FAA reference:** AFH emergency and traffic pattern chapters
- **Scenario-based:** Yes

## 9. How should you brief fire in flight in a piston single?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task I: Special Operations and Emergency Procedures
- **Model answer:** Aviate, then execute checklist memory items (mixture idle cutoff/fuel selector off/master as directed), initiate emergency descent/landing, communicate Mayday when able.
- **FAA reference:** POH emergency checklist; AFH

## 10. A student wants to launch despite “get-there” pressure for a family event. Which risk model best surfaces that hazard?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area II — Technical Subject Areas — Task I: Special Operations and Emergency Procedures
- **Model answer:** External pressure in PAVE and Passengers/Plan in 5P are key. Use DECIDE to create alternatives and objective no-go triggers before departure.
- **FAA reference:** PHAK Ch. 2; CFI ACS
- **Scenario-based:** Yes
