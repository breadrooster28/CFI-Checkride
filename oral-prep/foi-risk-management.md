# FOI: Risk Management

Total questions: 4

## 1. Define the PAVE checklist and give one risk example for each element.

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area I — Fundamentals of Instructing — Task F: Risk Management
- **Model answer:** Pilot, Aircraft, enVironment, External pressures. Example risks: pilot fatigue, deferred maintenance item, gusty crosswind, schedule pressure.
- **FAA reference:** PHAK FAA-H-8083-25 Ch. 2; CFI ACS

## 2. Explain the DECIDE model and how you teach it in real time.

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area I — Fundamentals of Instructing — Task F: Risk Management
- **Model answer:** Detect, Estimate, Choose, Identify, Do, Evaluate. Teach it by pausing during scenarios, verbalizing each step, and linking actions to outcomes and contingencies.
- **FAA reference:** PHAK FAA-H-8083-25 Ch. 2; AIH

## 3. What is the 5P model and when should it be evaluated?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area I — Fundamentals of Instructing — Task F: Risk Management
- **Model answer:** Plan, Plane, Pilot, Passengers, Programming; evaluate at preflight, takeoff, cruise, pre-descent, and before approach/landing, or whenever conditions change.
- **FAA reference:** PHAK FAA-H-8083-25 Ch. 2

## 4. How do you explain transfer of learning to a student transitioning aircraft?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area I — Fundamentals of Instructing — Task F: Risk Management
- **Model answer:** Positive transfer occurs when prior habits help; negative transfer when old habits interfere. Instructors identify differences explicitly and emphasize procedures unique to the new aircraft.
- **FAA reference:** AIH FAA-H-8083-9 Ch. 4
