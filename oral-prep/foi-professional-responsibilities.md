# FOI: Professional Responsibilities

Total questions: 4

## 1. What instructor responsibilities apply to endorsements and recordkeeping?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area I — Fundamentals of Instructing — Task E: Instructor Responsibilities and Professionalism
- **Model answer:** Instructors must ensure training meets applicable requirements, give accurate endorsements, maintain required records/signatures, and avoid endorsing applicants not prepared for safe operation.
- **FAA reference:** 14 CFR 61.189; 61.193; AC 61-65

## 2. How would you handle a learner who asks for a solo signoff but is not ready?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area I — Fundamentals of Instructing — Task E: Instructor Responsibilities and Professionalism
- **Model answer:** Decline endorsement, document deficiencies against standards, provide corrective training, and set objective criteria for reevaluation. Safety and regulatory compliance control the decision.
- **FAA reference:** AIH FAA-H-8083-9 Ch. 8; 14 CFR 61.87
- **Scenario-based:** Yes

## 3. What are signs of instructor professionalism during a checkride lesson presentation?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area I — Fundamentals of Instructing — Task E: Instructor Responsibilities and Professionalism
- **Model answer:** Accurate technical content, risk management integration, positive learning climate, clear standards, ethical conduct, and willingness to say “I will verify” when uncertain.
- **FAA reference:** CFI ACS Area I

## 4. What does “acceptance of responsibility” mean for a CFI applicant?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area I — Fundamentals of Instructing — Task E: Instructor Responsibilities and Professionalism
- **Model answer:** Owning the safety and quality of instruction: accurate teaching, legal endorsements, honest evaluations, and refusing unsafe or noncompliant operations despite external pressure.
- **FAA reference:** AIH FAA-H-8083-9 Ch. 8; CFI ACS
