# FOI: The Learning Process

Total questions: 4

## 1. Explain the laws of learning (REEPIR) with one practical cockpit example.

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area I — Fundamentals of Instructing — Task B: The Learning Process
- **Model answer:** Readiness, Exercise, Effect, Primacy, Intensity, Recency. Example: teaching checklist discipline correctly the first time (primacy), then repeated use (exercise) with immediate positive reinforcement (effect).
- **FAA reference:** AIH FAA-H-8083-9 Ch. 4

## 2. What are the levels of learning and how do you verify the highest level?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area I — Fundamentals of Instructing — Task B: The Learning Process
- **Model answer:** Rote, understanding, application, correlation. Correlation is shown when a learner applies knowledge in novel scenarios while prioritizing and making sound ADM decisions.
- **FAA reference:** AIH FAA-H-8083-9 Ch. 4
- **Gotcha / DPE follow-up:** Applicants often stop at “application” examples.

## 3. What causes forgetting, and what can instructors do to reduce it?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area I — Fundamentals of Instructing — Task B: The Learning Process
- **Model answer:** Forgetting is driven by disuse, interference, and fading memory traces. Reduce it with meaningful context, frequent review, scenario practice, and immediate correction of errors.
- **FAA reference:** AIH FAA-H-8083-9 Ch. 4

## 4. A learner keeps ballooning in the flare after multiple flights. Is this a plateaus issue or poor primacy, and what is your fix?

- **ACS Area/Task:** Area I — Fundamentals of Instructing — Task B: The Learning Process
- **Model answer:** Usually poor primacy or inconsistent cues, not just a plateau. Re-teach sight picture and timing from a stabilized approach profile, then deliberate practice with immediate feedback.
- **FAA reference:** AIH FAA-H-8083-9 Ch. 4; AFH Ch. 8
- **Scenario-based:** Yes
